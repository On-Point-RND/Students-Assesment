Introduction
Sleep staging is crucial for diagnosing sleep disorders.
Manual scoring of PSG is labor-intensive and inconsistent.
Automatic methods can increase accuracy and reduce workload.

Slide 3
----------------------------------------
Motivation
Need for accurate, interpretable, and efficient models.
ProductGraphSleepNet integrates spatial and temporal data using graph neural networks.

Slide 4
----------------------------------------
Limitations of Traditional Methods
Classical ML: Requires handcrafted features, lacks temporal modeling.
CNNs: Capture spatial but not temporal dependencies.
RNNs: Capture temporal but not spatial dependencies.

Slide 5
----------------------------------------
Multimodal Problem in Sleep Staging
Multimodal = Spatial + Temporal analysis.
EEG data across electrodes (spaatial).
Sleep stage sequences (temporal).
Requires integrated processing.

Slide 6
----------------------------------------
ProductGraphSleepNet Overview
A novel framework combining:
- Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs)
- Product Graph Learning (PGL)
- Spatial Attention (SpAtt)
- Temporal modeling via BiGRU
- Graph-wise Attention (GwAT)

Slide 7
----------------------------------------
Model Architecture
1. Spatial Attention Layer (SpAtt)
2. Product Graph Learning (PGL) Layer
3. Attentive Graph Convolution (AGC)
4. BiGRU for temporal transitions
5. Graph-wise Attention Network (GwAT)

Slide 8
----------------------------------------
Multimodal Integration
PGL fuses spatial and temporal graphs.
Allows modeling of interactions across brain regions and time.
Graph-based attention improves interpretability.
